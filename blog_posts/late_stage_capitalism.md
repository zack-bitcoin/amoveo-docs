Late Stage Capitalism
================

Late Stage Capitalism is the idea that our financial and political system was built on unstable foundations. That built-in inefficiencies are causing a buildup of technological debt, a kind of dead-wood that will lead to the system's inevitable self-destruction. Institutional rot destroying our systems from the inside. One-way changes to the rules governing our finances and politics, slowly cornering us. Trapped by the contradictions in our social contract.

Enclosure seperated people from their land, and created a dependent class that the elite could contol. Factories needed workers. Agricultural businesses wanted vast tracts of open land. So, the people were removed from the land and herded into cities. A land ownership system was built that benefited the large and easily taxed agricultural businesses, at the expense of smaller land plots owned by families. 

We have a broken monetary policy that optimizes for exploitation, rather than the economic security of the people. The gold standard is long gone. Money is lent into existence in a sick game of musical chairs, where a certain portion of players are guaranteed to fail every round. The bank takes interest from the winners, and it takes collateral from the losers, while ultimately providing nothing.

The political class is corrupt. They are openly receiving bribes. Harems of lobbiests stream into our capital. There are always legal ways for politicians to receive bribes. Whether it be jobs after they retire from politics, or investing tips from a market manipulator, or cash donations to the politician's non-profit. Shut off one stream of bribes, two more open. A politician who is focused on getting paid his next bribe, he isn't focused on making life better for his people.

Imperialism puts us all at risk. The military is serving the interests of big business instead of the interests of the citizens. Securing resources and maintaining rules for the financial security of the elite, while disregarding the physical security of the citizens.

These various factors are all harming our quality of life, and eroding the remaining reverence that people feel for our institutions, and they are harming our economic output. Sure, these factors might increase the taxable economy, or make some of the elite even richer. But the net impact of each one is harmful to the wider economy.
The live players will check-out to build the next generation of institutions from outside.
They have a low bar to clear. As we are losing economic efficiency, it is becoming easier to build an alternative that can grow faster.
